"I was 16 when I began collecting things that could not be touched. Missed conversations, half-written letters, unsent confessions, the strange ache of loving someone from a distance."

"I noticed that people spent fortunes on flowers that wilt in days. Yet the smallest message can remain alive for years. So I built a garden that does not die. A place where affection can bloom inside a link. Where a memory can be folded into a letter. Where a flower can carry a voice. Where a simple gift can become a tiny universe."

"I am not a florist. I do not arrange roses. I arrange feelings."
